There's more on my old youtube channel.



A 2009 short vfx video inspired by District9. I just put a spaceship in the sky, with some helicoptors.
Motiontracking the ship to the clouds was a very difficult process and had to be done by hand.



A 30 second titles pitch I got to do for a film in 2012.
Used maxscript to create motiongraphics components in 3d that were auto-rigged for faster production times.
Maxscript is a scripting language in 3ds Max, which runs ontop of .Net and C#.
Bonus: you can write and compile C# directly using maxscript. It's like python for C#, in 3d.



A 6 second animation I entered into an online animation competition in 2010.
Models were provided for all contestants, without any rigging. I did all rigging and animation.
In the middle of animation, I realized I could use the camera as a 3rd actor, to give the scene more life.
Making of here: https://youtu.be/XRz7Nssq230.



A test of a script/tool I wrote called Stretchy Bones Creator, in 2010.
The goal of the tool was to automate and simplify facial rigging as much as possible.
Here is a video showing the tool autorigging eyebrows: https://youtu.be/or7H-yADSxg.
Here is a video showing a full facial rig, rendered, controls, and bones:


The tool worked very well in most cases, and was largely based on the idea of 'avars', from Pixar.
You can learn more about avars here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avar_(animation_variable).

A 2011 tool that auto-builds droids based on parameters or rules.

A 2011 tool that merges pre-modeled and rigged characters, then applies animation files to them.
The entire character is then parented to a control on the ground, which the tool uses to manage the character.
This allowed me to produce tremendous amounts of work very quickly using characters and animations.
It was called Tiny because at the time crowd management was done by a tool called Massive, that was expensive.
